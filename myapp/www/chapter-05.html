Sight Words:
The High-Frequency Foundation

The words that unlock the speed and flow of reading

5.1
What Are Sight Words?

Sight words are words that appear extremely frequently in written English — so frequently that being able to read them instantly, without sounding them out, dramatically improves your reading speed and comprehension.

Some sight words follow regular phonics rules. Others contain irregular spellings that simply must be memorised.

The Big Idea
Why Sight Words Matter
The goal is not to skip phonics and memorise everything. The goal is to have a core of instantly recognisable words so that your mental energy is freed up to decode the newer, less familiar words you encounter.

Think of it this way: if you have to sound out "the", "and", "is", and "to" every time — your brain has no energy left to decode harder words. Sight words clear the runway so your brain can focus on what is new.
5.4 — The Four-Step Method
How to Learn a Sight Word

The most effective method combines multiple senses. Use all four steps for every new word. Tap each step to hear it explained.

1
SEE IT
Look at the word carefully. Notice any unusual or irregular spelling.
2
SAY IT
Say the word aloud clearly. Hear yourself say it.
3
SPELL IT
Spell it aloud letter by letter while looking at it.
4
WRITE IT
Write it from memory. Check your spelling. Repeat if wrong.
