11.1
What Is a Morpheme?

A morpheme is the smallest unit of meaning in language. It is not the smallest sound (that is a phoneme) and not the smallest letter (that is a grapheme). A morpheme is the smallest chunk of language that carries meaning on its own.

The Big Idea
One Word — Three Morphemes
The word unhappiness contains three morphemes:

un- (prefix: "not")  +  happy (root: "glad")  +  -ness (suffix: "state of")

= the state of not being glad

Learn the parts — decode any word.
Three Types
Root · Prefix · Suffix
①Root Word: The base carrying the core meaning. All other morphemes attach to it. act, port, vis, graph, struct
②Prefix: Added to the front of a root — changes its meaning. un-, re-, pre-, dis-, trans-
③Suffix: Added to the end of a root — changes its grammatical function or meaning. -tion, -ness, -er, -ly, -able
