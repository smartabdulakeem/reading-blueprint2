16.1
Beyond Decoding

Decoding is necessary but not sufficient for reading. You can decode every word in a passage and still fail to understand what it means.

Comprehension is the purpose of reading. Decoding is the doorway; comprehension is the room you enter.

The Big Idea
Five Levels — Moving Deeper
Reading researchers have identified five levels of understanding. A skilled reader moves through all of them — often simultaneously.

Level 1 — Literal: What the text directly says.
Level 2 — Inferential: What the text implies but does not state.
Level 3 — Structural: How the text is organised.
Level 4 — Evaluative: Whether the text is accurate or well-argued.
Level 5 — Applied: How the text connects to life, other texts, or the world.

16.3 — The SQ3R Method
A Reading System for Deep Understanding

SQ3R is a structured approach to reading non-fiction texts for deep understanding. It prevents passive reading — the kind where your eyes move across the page but your mind absorbs nothing.

Five Steps — Tap Any Row to Hear It
StepNameWhat to Do
SQ3R in Practice
Apply It to Any Non-Fiction Text
SSurvey first: Before reading, scan the title, headings, first and last paragraphs. Build a mental map.
QTurn headings into questions: "The Causes of War" becomes "What caused this war?" Write them down.
R1Read actively: Look for answers to your questions. Underline key sentences. Mark unfamiliar words.
R2Recite after each section: Close the text. Answer your question from memory. This is the most powerful step.
R3Review the whole text: Re-read your questions and answers. Re-read anything you could not recite. Write a brief summary.
