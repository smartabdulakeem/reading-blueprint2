18.1
Why Vocabulary Is the Engine of Reading

You can decode every word in a sentence and still not understand it — if you do not know what the words mean. Vocabulary is the engine of comprehension.

Research consistently shows that a reader's vocabulary size is one of the strongest predictors of reading comprehension, academic success, and lifelong earning potential. Word wealth is real wealth.

The Big Idea
Systems Over Lists
The old approach: memorise long lists of words and definitions.
The result: words forgotten within a week.

The smart approach: understand the system — tiers, morphemes, context clues, and word maps. Build vocabulary organically through reading. Words learned in context stick for life.

18.2 — The Four Vocabulary Tiers
Not All Words Are Equal

Vocabulary researchers have organised all words into four tiers based on frequency, specificity, and instructional value. Understanding the tiers tells you which words to focus on first.

Why Tier 2 Is the Priority
The Sweet Spot for Learning

Tier 2 words are found across all subject areas and academic texts. They appear in science, history, literature, business, and law — but are rarely taught directly. A student who knows Tier 2 words deeply has a massive advantage in every subject.

Examples of Tier 2 words that unlock understanding: analyse, consequence, contrast, evaluate, infer, justify, significant, subsequent, accumulate, ambiguous.

How to Make Your Own Word Map
Five Steps
1Write the target word in the centre. Say it aloud three times.
2Write the part of speech and a clear, simple definition in your own words.
3Write an original sentence using the word in a meaningful context.
4List related words and forms (tenacious → tenacity, tenaciously).
5List antonyms — knowing what a word is NOT deepens your understanding of what it IS.
