13.1
What Makes a Sentence?

A sentence is a complete thought. Every sentence must have two essential elements: a subject (who or what the sentence is about) and a predicate (what the subject does or is). Without both, you do not have a complete sentence.

The Big Idea
Four Types · Four Structures
Every sentence in English is one of four types (based on purpose) and one of four structures (based on how clauses are combined).

Types: Declarative · Interrogative · Imperative · Exclamatory
Structures: Simple · Compound · Complex · Compound-Complex

13.3 — Subject and Predicate
The Two Essential Parts

Every sentence has two essential parts: the subject (who or what the sentence is about) and the predicate (what the subject does or is). Finding these two parts unlocks the meaning of even the most complex sentence.

Subject = the gold part — who or what the sentence is about.
Predicate = the green part — everything the sentence says about the subject.
