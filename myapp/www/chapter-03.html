3.1

The CVC Pattern

CVC stands for Consonant–Vowel–Consonant. It is the most fundamental word pattern in beginning English reading — the pattern that will unlock your first 500 words.

C — V — C
Consonant · Vowel · Consonant
The CVC Rule: In a CVC word, the middle vowel always makes its SHORT sound.
cat
/k/ + /a/ + /t/
sit
/s/ + /i/ + /t/
hop
/h/ + /o/ + /p/
bed
/b/ + /e/ + /d/
run
/r/ + /u/ + /n/
How to Read a CVC Word

Step by Step

1Find the middle letter — is it A, E, I, O, or U?
2That is your vowel. It makes its short sound.
3Sound out from left to right using Controlled Blending.
4Blend all three sounds together to read the word.
