7.1
What Is a Digraph?

A digraph is two letters that together make one completely new sound — a sound that neither letter makes on its own.

This is the most important distinction in Phase 2: a blend keeps each sound separate, but a digraph fuses two letters into one brand-new sound.

The Critical Distinction
Blend vs Digraph — One More Time
BLEND
black
Each letter keeps its own sound.
bl = /b/ + /l/ — both heard.
DIGRAPH
ship
Two letters → one new sound.
sh = /sh/ — neither original sound remains.

7.2 — The TH Digraph
TH — Two Different Sounds

TH is the only major digraph in English that has two sounds: a voiced version and an unvoiced version. Both are made by placing your tongue between your teeth.

Voiced vs Unvoiced TH
Feel the Difference in Your Throat
Test: Place two fingers gently on your throat.
Say "this" — you feel vibration → Voiced TH.
Say "thin" — you feel no vibration → Unvoiced TH.
