10.1
Why Silent Letters Exist

English has borrowed words from Latin, Greek, French, Norse, and dozens of other languages. Silent letters are often historical artifacts — letters that were once pronounced in the original language but fell silent as the word was absorbed into English.

Understanding this history removes the frustration: silent letters are not mistakes. They are the fingerprints of history.

The Big Idea
Pattern Over Memorisation
Most silent letters follow predictable patterns. You do not need to memorise every word individually. Once you learn the pattern — for example, K is silent before N at the start of a word — you can apply that rule to every word that fits.
