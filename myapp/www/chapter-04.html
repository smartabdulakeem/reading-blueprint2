4.1
What Makes a Vowel Long?

When a vowel is long, it says its name — the same sound you use when reciting the alphabet.

Long A says "ay."   Long E says "ee."   Long I says "eye."   Long O says "oh."   Long U says "you."

The Big Idea
Short Vowels vs. Long Vowels
In Chapter 3, you learned that CVC words contain a short vowel — the vowel is trapped and kept short by the consonant after it.

In this chapter, you will learn the two main patterns that make a vowel go long:
1. The Silent E — a final E flips the vowel from short to long.
2. Vowel Teams — two vowels side by side team up to make one long sound.

Mastering these two patterns doubles the words you can read.
Two Patterns That Change Everything
How Long Vowels Are Made
1Silent E: A final silent E makes the previous vowel say its long name.  cap → cape  |  pin → pine  |  hop → hope
2Vowel Teams: Two vowels side by side often produce one long sound.  rain, feet, boat, moon

4.3 — Pattern 2
Vowel Teams

When two vowels appear side by side, they often team up to produce one single long vowel sound.

Memory Rule: "When two vowels go walking, the first one does the talking."
The first vowel says its long name. The second vowel is usually silent.
