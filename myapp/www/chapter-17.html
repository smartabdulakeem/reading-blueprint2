17.1
What Is Prosody?

Prosody is the rhythm, stress, pitch, and intonation of spoken language. It is the music of reading aloud. A reader with good prosody does not just decode words correctly — they make the text come alive, reflecting meaning through vocal performance.

Prosody is the bridge between accurate reading and fluent reading.

The Big Idea
Prosody = Comprehension Made Audible
Research shows that prosody and comprehension are deeply connected. Readers who read with appropriate prosody almost always comprehend better than those who read in a flat, word-by-word monotone.

Why? Because prosody reflects understanding. You cannot read with natural rhythm and expression if you do not understand what you are reading. The music reveals the meaning — to both reader and listener.

17.3 — Sentence Stress Patterns
Content Words vs Function Words

In English, content words (nouns, main verbs, adjectives, adverbs) are typically stressed. Function words (articles, prepositions, auxiliary verbs, conjunctions, pronouns) are typically unstressed — spoken quickly and quietly.

Content words carry the meaning — they get the stress.
Function words carry the grammar — they stay quiet.

Example: "The TALL MAN WALKED SLOWLY down the NARROW STREET."
the, down, the → unstressed.  tall, man, walked, slowly, narrow, street → stressed.
