15.1
The Anatomy of a Paragraph

A paragraph is the fundamental unit of organised writing. Every well-formed paragraph has three elements: a topic sentence that announces the main idea, body sentences that develop and support that idea, and a concluding sentence that wraps up the paragraph or transitions to the next.

The Big Idea
Three Parts — One Unit of Thought
Think of a paragraph like a sandwich:

🍞 Topic sentence — the top slice: announces what is inside.
🥗 Body sentences — the filling: the evidence, examples, and explanation.
🍞 Concluding sentence — the bottom slice: holds it together and completes the thought.

Without any one of the three, the sandwich — the paragraph — falls apart.
