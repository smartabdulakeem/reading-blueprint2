9.1
Why Syllables Matter

A syllable is the smallest unit of pronunciation that contains a vowel sound. Every word can be broken into syllables, and every syllable belongs to one of six types.

Knowing the syllable type tells you exactly how to pronounce the vowel in that syllable. This is not a trivial skill — it is the master key to pronouncing any English word you encounter for the first time.

The Big Idea
Six Types — One System
Every syllable in the English language fits into one of six types. Once you know the type, you know the vowel sound — automatically. No guessing required.

9.2 — The Chin Test
How to Count Syllables

The Chin Test is a simple physical technique for counting syllables in any word. It works because every syllable contains one vowel sound — and every vowel sound makes your chin drop slightly as you say it.

Try It — Tap a Word and Count the Drops

Tap a Word — Count Your Chin Drops
Chin Test Steps
Four Steps
1Place the palm of your hand flat under your chin.
2Say the word clearly at normal speed.
3Count how many times your chin drops and touches your hand.
4Each chin-drop is one syllable.
9.5 — Division Rules
VCCV and VCV Rules

Knowing the syllable types helps you know where to divide a word. There are two main division rules.

VCCV Rule: When two consonants appear between two vowels, divide between the consonants.
rab|bit  ·  bas|ket  ·  nap|kin  ·  den|tist

VCV Rule: When one consonant appears between two vowels, usually divide before the consonant — giving the first syllable a long vowel (open syllable).
ba|by  ·  o|pen  ·  mu|sic  ·  si|lent
