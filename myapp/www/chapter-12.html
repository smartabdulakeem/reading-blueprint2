12.1
Why Grammar Matters for Reading

Understanding grammar is not about memorising rules to please a teacher. It is about understanding how meaning is encoded in the structure of a sentence. When you know the function of each word in a sentence, you can understand more complex texts, catch ambiguity, and write clearly and powerfully.

The Big Idea
Eight Parts — One System
Every word in every English sentence belongs to one of eight categories called the Parts of Speech. Each category has a specific job — a function it performs in the sentence. Understanding these functions is what separates a reader who decodes words from a reader who truly understands what they are reading.
