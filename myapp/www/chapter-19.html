19.1
The Expert Reader

An expert reader is not simply someone who reads quickly or makes no errors. An expert reader is someone who reads strategically, critically, and adaptively — choosing the right strategies for the right texts, monitoring their own understanding, and adjusting their approach when comprehension breaks down.

Expert reading is a learnable skill — not a talent you are born with.

The Big Idea
Reading Is Thinking Made Visible
Reading and thinking are the same activity. When you read deeply, you are not passively receiving information — you are actively constructing meaning, questioning the author, connecting ideas, and evaluating arguments.

The difference between a weak reader and an expert reader is not intelligence. It is strategy.

19.4 — Critical Reading
Reading That Pushes Back

Critical reading means evaluating what you read rather than simply absorbing it. A critical reader asks: Is this claim supported? Is this reasoning valid? Is there a perspective missing? Who benefits from this argument?

19.5 — The Daily Reading Blueprint
Five Habits for Lifelong Literacy

Literacy is not a destination — it is a practice. The readers who grow most rapidly are those who have made reading a daily non-negotiable habit. These five habits, practised consistently, will transform any reader into an expert within one year.

A Reading Practice Plan
Structuring Your Week
MonFiction: At least 20 minutes. Read for pleasure — build fluency and love of reading.
TueNon-fiction: Use SQ3R. Annotate. Practise all five comprehension levels.
WedVocabulary: Review new words from the week. Make word maps for two new Tier 2 words.
ThuAloud: Read something aloud for 10 minutes. Focus on prosody and expression.
FriCritical: Read a news article or essay. Apply critical reading questions.
SatReview: Dicto-Comp with a short passage. Review your annotations from the week.
SunFree read: Read whatever you want — whatever makes you excited to open a book.
