6.1
What Is a Consonant Blend?

A consonant blend is two or three consonants that appear together in a word, where each consonant keeps its own individual sound. The sounds are not merged — they are blended together quickly and smoothly.

The Blend Test
How to Identify a Blend
Say the word "black" slowly: /b/ ... /l/ ... /a/ ... /k/
You can still hear both /b/ and /l/ separately. That is a blend.

Now compare with a digraph: say "ship" — you do NOT hear /s/ and /h/ separately. You hear one new sound: /sh/. That is a digraph — not a blend.

Blend = each letter keeps its sound.  |  Digraph = two letters make one new sound.
Blend vs Digraph — Side by Side
See the Difference
BLEND
